Discover Video Showcase

Advanced Instructions

We invite researchers of all levels to create videos about their research that is intended for general audience on a wide-range of topics covering the spectrum of research and scholarship at UVA. Here are the submissions so far.

Videos depicting research and scholarship from all areas and disciplines welcome.

If you don't have your own youtube channel and don't intend to make one please follow the basic instructions found here. The instructions below are for those that want their video hosted on their own independent youtube channel.

Basic Instructions

Submission Criteria

  • Videos may be from 30 seconds up to three minutes long describing the work.
  • A wide range of modalities are allowable from a simple narration of the work in front of a camera, a PowerPoint presentation with audio, a video production showing results or important artifacts from the research or work in progress, and others that the presenters can imagine to effectively communicate their work to general audience.
  • Top three will be highlighted at the Discovery event October 19, 2018.
  • There is no “judging”. This is simply a forum for researchers to showcase their research and receive feedback on how well the message is received, understood, and liked by general audience through direct feedback about your video and the likes and web engagement.
  • All videos must be submitted by Friday, October 12, 2018 to be considered for the event.
  • For more information visit the profiles of discovery site or email Cheryl Wagner Cheryl-vpr@Virginia.edu

Looking for more control over your video submission?

Do you have your own youtube channel you'd like to post the video on? Do you want to improve your video with advanced youtube features like annotations, end cards, and subtitles? If so, please contact us by email or by using the form below. Taking advandtage of these additional features will require your research group to have their own youtube account and abide by some UVA rules. If this option interests your group, here are some of the pros and cons of submitting your video this way.

Pros
  • Improve your showcase video with extra youtube features - You can add annotations, end cards, subtitles, links to your group's website and other annimations to improve your video.
  • Build your own youtube presense - The video description will display as your own youtube channel so you can direct traffic and interest to your group instead of the video showing as posted by the Vice President for Research at UVA Youtube Channel You can post future videos to the channel you create and grow your own online presence. All views your video gets as a result of this showcase will be attributed to your channel which will increase your channels prominense in search results.
  • You will be able to better credit the creators of the video on your channel.
Cons
  • Extra work - Setting up your own channel will require some additional work on your part.
  • Channel must be UVA/research branded - You can't post your video to your personal youtube channel. We don't want you uploading the showcase video to your personal youtube channel where if people click on the channel they will see your playlists of your favorite cat videos or music. The channel will have to be connected to your reserach and uva only. Take a look at the UVA youtube channel and the Vice President for Research at UVA youtube channel for examples of what is required of UVA branding. It's essentially just a UVA image as your profile image and a UVA or research related picture in your channel art. Most of what we're asking is that you don't have unrelated content cluttering your channel

How to submit your video hosted on your own youtube channel

Please please upload your video and then use this collaboration link to add the showcase playlist to your channel. Then you will be able to add your video to the playlist. Please email us at uvaresearchvp@gmail.com and use the form below to let us know that you've added your video. You can check that you've done it right by checking the youtube playlist at the top of this page or by visiting the playlist directly and looking for your video.
